Details

I have used 1 breakpoint and 1 tweak point:
Breakpoint: @media screen and (min-width: 600px)
I wish to have the same layout and design for mobile devices with 600px as mobile devices with 300px so only one breakpoint is applied.
600px was chosen because when the screen gets smaller than 600px, fonts will be too small for users to see so I wish to fix the font-size for devices which are less than 600px.
For mobile devices, pictures in homepage, contact page and degree programme will be below texts rather than aside with texts.
The 2 rows and 2 columns of content of the homepage will become 1 row and 4 columns in desktop devices.
Words will be different at breakpoints. For mobile devices, words is fixed at 18px, h1 is 50px, h2 is 28px and h3-h6 are 24px.
For desktop devices (above 600px), words will get bigger as resolution increases as viewport width is used.
The margin-left of footer bar will be different as well which is margin-left: 0% for mobile devices and 5% for desktop devices.
A menu (dropdown) system will be present in mobile devices where lists of header navigation will drop down when users hover over it and the menu system will become normal navigation bar in desktop devices which I did not use any of the examples given in the assignment instructions.

Tweak point: @media screen and (max-width: 500px) is for form where words will stack over input column and submit button will cover the whole row.
This is because when the screen gets to 500px, labels and input areas will be too close together which is not that good to look at. The rest of the web pages will be similar (SGA & mySkills, Picture quiz, Accessibility, Legal & Security, Design, Testing).
