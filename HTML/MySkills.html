SGA & mySkills

What's SGA

The Sheffield Graduate Attributes (SGA) is a presentation of personal attributes and abilities which the university community and external partners have agreed are likely to be particularly beneficial to academic success, personal development and employability. This helps students to achieve what they are seeking and to adapt to the changing world. Students are encouraged to consider their personal development goals and reflects their strengths using mySkills mySkills portfolio

SGA is grouped into three sections:

My learning
Skills to learn and apply knowledge effectively
Categories: Academic skills, applying knowledge, critical thinking etc.
My impact
Ability to communicate and collaborate effectively with other people within communities
Awareness of impact of ethical, societal and environmental decisions
Categories: Working with others, equality and inclusion, sustainability etc.
My self
Ability to develop and maintain positive mind which is crucial for lifelong wellbeing
Categories: Personal development, positive wellbeing, enterprising etc.

For more information about SGA, please visit: SGA

What's mySkills

An innovative new way for students to assess, record, build and reflect on your own skills profile. A student's profile and record of personal development will be unique and will be invaluable in creating your CV and strengthening job applications and interview performance.

The first thing to do is a ' skills assessment ' of some of your current skills and attributes through a multiple-choice questionnaire which consists of 4 statements for each question. Roughly takes around 20 minutes to complete, be honest when you're doing it
Student will see a profile based on his/her answers which highlights their strengths, and he/she will be able to choose which attributes to enhance.
Student can then start to record his/her experiences and personal development.

Accessing mySkills
Students can access mySkills through Career Connect

For more information about mySkills, please visit: mySkills

My experience

  1. During lectures, we are always given opportunities to think and work out on our own before solutions are given which allows me to train my problem-solving skills. Our COM1002 Foundation of Computer Science always needs us to think to fully understand the logic principles behind and I have learnt a lot on how to apply these principles when solving different questions
  2. The university always organise campaigns, webinars and workshops to increase our awareness on equality and inclusion which helps me to be able to adapt into the international community easier which is undeniably great for international students.
  3. The university has a lot of facilities which allow students to carry out their research and innovation. For instance, IForge which allows students to create their own things, is certainly one of the most interesting highlights. The diamond also has state-of-the-art facilities and equipment for students to have hands-on experience.
